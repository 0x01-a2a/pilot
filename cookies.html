Cookie Policy

Effective Date: March 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how the 0x01 website (0x01.world) uses cookies and similar technologies. The short version: we keep it minimal.

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide basic analytics to site owners.

2. What We Use

The 0x01 website is a static site. We do not deploy advertising networks, social tracking pixels, or third-party analytics platforms that set persistent cookies.

The only cookies or local storage that may be set are:

  • Essential / functional: Necessary for basic site navigation and to remember any preferences you explicitly set on the site (e.g., dismissing a banner). These are session-scoped and expire when you close your browser.
  • Font loading: Google Fonts is used for typography. Google may set cookies per their own privacy policy. No personally identifiable information is shared with us as a result.

3. Mobile Application

The 0x01 Pilot mobile application does not use browser cookies. It uses Android's local storage mechanisms (SharedPreferences, Android Keystore) to persist configuration data on your device only. This data never leaves your device except as part of explicitly initiated network requests to your configured node API.

4. Third-Party Services

Links from our website may lead to third-party services (GitHub, NPM, Solana explorers, etc.) that operate under their own cookie policies. We recommend reviewing the privacy and cookie policies of any third-party site you visit.

5. Your Choices

You can configure your browser to reject cookies or to alert you when cookies are being set. Note that disabling all cookies may affect the functionality of some websites, though it should not materially affect your use of this static documentation site.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy if we introduce new features that change our data practices. The "Effective Date" above will be updated accordingly.

7. Contact

Questions? Reach us at privacy@0x01.world.
